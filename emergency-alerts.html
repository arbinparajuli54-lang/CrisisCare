Emergency Alerts & Key Contacts

Quick access to critical public service numbers including police, fire, ambulance, disaster management, and health helplines.

In an emergency, stay calm, move to a safe place, and call the most relevant number below.

Primary emergency numbers

Police

100 / 112

For crimes, violence, missing persons, or immediate law and order issues.

Ambulance & Medical

108 / 102

For medical emergencies, accidents, or serious health issues needing urgent care.

Fire & Rescue

101

For fires, building collapses, chemical leaks, and rescue operations.

Additional public services

Disaster Management

1070 / 1077

State and district level control rooms for natural disasters.

Health Helpline

104

Basic health advice and information (varies by state/country).

Women’s Helpline

181

Support for women facing violence, abuse, or harassment.

Child Helpline

1098

Emergency support for children in distress.

Senior Citizen Helpline

14567

Assistance for elderly persons in crisis (where available).

How to call for help effectively

  1. Identify your location clearly – nearest landmark, street, or building name.
  2. Explain what happened in one or two clear sentences.
  3. Tell them how many people are involved and if anyone is seriously injured.
  4. Follow operator instructions and do not hang up until told to do so.

Numbers mentioned here are common examples used in many regions (like India) and may vary by country. Always verify official helplines for your specific location.
