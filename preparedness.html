Preparedness for Disasters

Learn what to do before, during, and after common emergencies such as fires, earthquakes, and floods. Use this as a starting guide along with official local instructions.

Fire Safety

Before a fire (Pre‑event)

  • Install smoke alarms on every floor and test them every month.
  • Keep fire extinguishers easily accessible in kitchens and near exits.
  • Plan at least two escape routes from every room in your home or building.

During a fire (Ongoing)

  • Stay low and crawl under smoke to the nearest safe exit.
  • Never use elevators during a fire; use stairs only.
  • If your clothes catch fire, stop, drop, and roll until the flames are out.

After a fire (Post‑event)

  • Do not re‑enter the building until authorities say it is safe.
  • Seek medical attention for any breathing difficulty or burns.
  • Document damages for insurance and relief authorities if applicable.

Earthquake Safety

Before an earthquake (Pre‑event)

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, and medicines for at least 72 hours.
  • Identify safe spots such as under sturdy tables or against interior walls.

During an earthquake (Ongoing)

  • Drop, cover, and hold on under a sturdy table or desk.
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects that may fall.
  • If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.

After an earthquake (Post‑event)

  • Expect aftershocks and be prepared to drop, cover, and hold on again.
  • Check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid if needed.
  • Follow evacuation instructions from local authorities and avoid damaged structures.

Flood Safety

Before a flood (Pre‑event)

  • Know if you live in a flood‑prone area and learn local evacuation routes.
  • Store important documents in waterproof containers or digital backups.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with clean water, dry food, and essential medicines.

During a flood (Ongoing)

  • Move to higher ground immediately and avoid walking through moving water.
  • Do not attempt to drive through flooded roads; just 30 cm of water can float a car.
  • Listen to local radio or official alerts for evacuation and shelter information.

After a flood (Post‑event)

  • Avoid standing water as it may be electrically charged or contaminated.
  • Boil or treat drinking water until authorities confirm it is safe.
  • Photograph damage for relief applications and insurance if available.
